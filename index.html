Vitband

UIUX Design

Overview

VITBAND is an AI-powered fitness wearable designed to help athletes better understand muscle activation, fatigue, and recovery throughout their training. The project focused on creating an intuitive mobile experience that transforms complex biometric data into actionable insights, making performance tracking easier to understand and more motivating for everyday users.

My Role

As the UI/UX Designer, I was responsible for designing user-centered experiences across the mobile application while collaborating closely with the product team throughout the design process.

Responsibilities

  • Designed user flows and mobile UI for key features including Dashboard, History, and AI Coach.
  • Created wireframes, high-fidelity interfaces, and interaction prototypes in Figma.
  • Developed visual assets and marketing materials to maintain a consistent brand identity.
  • Conducted competitor analysis across fitness wearables and health tracking applications, identifying opportunities to improve usability and information hierarchy.
  • Worked with the team to simplify complex performance metrics into more intuitive visualizations.
  • Redesigned the muscle energy system from a countdown model (100% -> 0%) to a progress-based model (0% -> 100%), creating a more rewarding and motivating user experience.

Research

Before designing the product, we explored the current fitness wearable market and interviewed potential users to better understand their training habits and expectations.

Our research focused on three key questions:

  • What information do athletes care about most during training?
  • How can complex biometric data be translated into simple, actionable insights?
  • How can AI coaching create a more engaging training experience instead of simply displaying numbers?

We also evaluated products such as Polar, WHOOP, and Aura Ring to understand their interaction patterns, information architecture, and wearable experience. Based on these findings, we explored opportunities to create a more approachable and motivating experience.

Insight 01

Users wanted more than raw performance data. They expected clear explanations and practical recommendations that could help improve future workouts.

Insight 02

Traditional dashboards often felt overly technical. Participants preferred a cleaner interface with stronger visual hierarchy and easier navigation between key features.

Insight 03

Muscle fatigue was consistently ranked as one of the most valuable metrics. Users wanted to understand when to push harder and when to prioritize recovery.

Insight 04

Displaying muscle energy as something that grows over time felt significantly more motivating than watching it decrease throughout a workout.

Creative Concept

Design Goal

Transform complex physiological data into an experience that feels intuitive, motivating, and actionable.

Design Principles

  • Make performance data easy to understand at a glance.
  • Prioritize the most important metrics through stronger visual hierarchy.
  • Replace technical language with conversational AI coaching.
  • Encourage long-term engagement through positive feedback and progress visualization.
  • Build a clean, modern visual language inspired by premium fitness technology while remaining approachable for everyday users.

Dashboard

The dashboard was designed to transform complex biomechanical data into an experience that users could understand within seconds.

Instead of overwhelming athletes with technical metrics, we prioritized the most actionable information through a clear visual hierarchy, progressive disclosure, and simple data visualization. The goal was to help users quickly assess their physical condition before deciding whether to push harder, recover, or adjust their training intensity.

Each metric was intentionally grouped around a single question: How is my body performing today?

AI Coach

The AI Coach was designed to bridge the gap between performance data and meaningful action.

Rather than acting as a traditional chatbot, the experience functions as a personalized training companion that interprets physiological data and delivers context-aware recommendations in natural language. The objective was to reduce the effort required for users to understand their performance while increasing confidence in training decisions.

Instead of asking users to interpret charts themselves, the AI transforms data into personalized guidance.

Key Design Decisions

  • Prioritized high-value metrics such as fatigue, muscle activation, and load balance.
  • Used expandable cards to progressively reveal detailed analytics without increasing cognitive load.
  • Simplified scientific terminology into language that everyday athletes could understand.
  • Created a soft visual system using gradients and generous spacing to make health data feel approachable rather than clinical.
  • Highlighted trends instead of isolated numbers, encouraging users to focus on long-term performance rather than daily fluctuations.

Key Design Decisions

  • Introduced suggested prompts to reduce decision fatigue and help users start conversations naturally.
  • Added conversation history, allowing users to revisit previous recommendations and monitor long-term progress.
  • Designed quick actions such as Workout Suggestion, Soreness Prediction, Tips, and Make a Plan to support common user goals with a single tap.
  • Focused on conversational language instead of technical terminology to improve accessibility.
  • Built a flexible interaction model where AI recommendations adapt to users' latest performance metrics, creating a more personalized coaching experience.

Heytea Rebrand

Non-commercial Case Study

Introduction

The concept is Liquor + Tea. By blending the sophisticated flavor of spirits with the rich cultural heritage of tea, HEYTEA stands out in a saturated beverage market while creating a more sophisticated and historical brand experience.

Values & Positioning

Values

The brand offers high-quality, culturally deep tea and liquor blended beverages. By blending traditional tea art with modern liquor craft, it focuses on the purity of ingredients, elegance of vibe, and a relaxing and enjoyable experience for consumers.

Positioning

HEYTEA is positioned as an innovative and powerful beverage brand for young consumers looking for fresh experience and cultural depth. The brand combines Chinese art aesthetics with modern minimalist design to convey an elegant and pure brand image.

Tone

Elegant

The brand's elegant style brings a sense of refinement and premium lifestyle to consumers.

Traditional

The visual direction is rooted in Chinese tea culture, giving HEYTEA a deeper cultural context.

Pure

A pure tone communicates simplicity, clarity, and the quality of the ingredients.

Relaxable

The relaxed atmosphere gives consumers a feeling of comfort, serenity, and enjoyment.

Visualization

The visualization system is built from the logo, typography, color palette, graphic elements, images, and interface applications. Each part supports the Liquor + Tea concept through a minimal black, white, and gray visual language.

Logo

The logo expresses our products very clearly and directly. At the same time, the logo is simple, modern and elegant. It can be used in many different contexts, such as product packaging, posters and advertisements.

The logo design is inspired by the shape of ancient Chinese wine altars and modern milk tea cups. The serif typeface gives the brand a more elegant and historical character.

Typography

Didot is used as the primary headline typeface. Aa WuDaoJia supports the slogan and calligraphic feeling, while Inter is used for body copy, legal text, and app interface information.

Color

The colors are inspired by traditional Chinese ink paintings, with black and white as the main base and a range of grays used in the illustrations to highlight the hierarchy of the design. All secondary colors can be flexibly applied to all illustration elements.

Elements

Wine jars, bottles, wine glasses, teacups, teapots and other containers are used for tea and liquor. Mountains are the most frequent element in ink paintings, and they also exist as a secondary graphic in our visual system. Mountains can be irregular and random.

Posters

Posters are used to draw the brand image to consumers. The visual language keeps the same logo, illustration elements, slogan, and black-white-gray structure.

Physical Applications

The physical applications include packaging, paper bags, cups, stickers, straws, and business cards. The brand system is applied through clean surfaces, large logo marks, and the same black-white-gray illustration language.
